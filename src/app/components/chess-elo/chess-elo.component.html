Who stole the show this week at London's Annual 'Bobby Fischer' chess tournament?


By: Zach Alexander

(This is a mock news story for the first project in my DATA 607 graduate school course.)

London was bustling this week as thousands of excited fans flocked to the annual Bobby Fischer chess tournament. After much anticipation going into the week, one player in particular was stealing the headlines.

Gary Hua, a native to Vancouver, had been unstoppable going into the week. With a pre-tournament elo rating of 1794, which surpassed all others in the field, he would be a tough opponent to beat.

Those immediately following behind him on the leaderboard also showed promise, including the young 19 year-old phenom, Patrick Schilling. Raised from a modest upbringing, he's been touted as one of the most exciting new players to enter the field.

Although both of these players had promising outlooks, #2 ranked Loren Schwiebert had an advantage when it came to strength of schedule. From the chart below you can see that Loren's opponents going into the tournament had a much lower average pre-tournament elo rating than the opponents of Gary Hua or Patrick Schilling.
